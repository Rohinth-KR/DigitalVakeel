MSME SAMADHAAN — DELAYED PAYMENT MONITORING SYSTEM
=====================================================

WHAT IS MSME SAMADHAAN?
-------------------------
MSME Samadhaan is an online portal launched by the Ministry of Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises, Government of India, to help MSMEs file complaints about delayed payments from buyers. The portal empowers micro and small entrepreneurs to directly register their cases of delayed payments.

Portal URL: https://samadhaan.msme.gov.in
Also accessible via: https://msme.gov.in → Samadhaan link

The system connects:
- MSME suppliers (who file complaints)
- Micro and Small Enterprise Facilitation Councils (MSEFCs) 
- Buyers (who are required to respond)

ELIGIBILITY TO FILE ON SAMADHAAN
----------------------------------
Who can file:
1. Only Micro and Small Enterprises (NOT medium enterprises for Samadhaan)
2. Must have valid Udyam Registration (earlier known as Udyog Aadhaar/EM-II)
3. The supplier must have supplied goods or rendered services
4. Payment must be overdue beyond 45 days (or agreed date, whichever is earlier)

Who can you file against:
- Any buyer — private companies, public sector undertakings, government departments
- The buyer can be any entity regardless of size
- Government buyers are also covered

Documents needed for filing:
1. Udyam Registration Certificate/Number
2. Copy of the invoice(s)
3. Proof of supply/delivery (delivery challan, acknowledgment)
4. Copy of purchase order/contract
5. Any communication about payment (emails, letters)
6. Details of the buyer (name, address, contact)

STEP-BY-STEP PROCESS TO FILE ON MSME SAMADHAAN
-------------------------------------------------

STEP 1: REGISTER ON THE PORTAL
- Go to https://samadhaan.msme.gov.in
- Click "Entrepreneur (Supplier)" → Registration
- Enter your Udyam Registration Number
- Fill in your enterprise details (name, address, contact, etc.)
- Create login credentials (username and password)
- Verify your mobile number and email via OTP

STEP 2: LOGIN AND FILE A NEW CASE
- Login with your credentials
- Click "File Application" or "New Case"
- Select the state/district where the buyer is located
- This determines which Facilitation Council will handle the case

STEP 3: FILL CASE DETAILS
- Enterprise details (auto-filled from Udyam registration)
- Buyer details:
  - Name and address of the buyer company
  - Contact person and designation
  - Email and phone number
  - GSTIN of the buyer (if available)
- Invoice details:
  - Invoice number and date
  - Amount of each invoice
  - Date of supply/delivery
  - Agreed payment terms
  - Amount outstanding (including interest)

STEP 4: UPLOAD SUPPORTING DOCUMENTS
- Upload invoices (PDF/scanned copies)
- Upload delivery proof
- Upload purchase order/contract
- Upload any payment reminders sent
- Upload any partial payment receipts (if applicable)

STEP 5: CALCULATE AND CLAIM INTEREST
- The portal may calculate interest automatically
- You can claim interest under Section 16 at 3× RBI bank rate
- Enter the number of days the payment is delayed
- The system calculates the interest amount

STEP 6: SUBMIT THE APPLICATION
- Review all details entered
- Submit the application
- You will receive a case number / reference number
- Save this number for future reference

WHAT HAPPENS AFTER FILING?
-----------------------------

Stage 1: NOTICE TO BUYER (within 15-30 days)
- The Facilitation Council sends a notice to the buyer
- Buyer is asked to respond within a specified time
- The notice includes a copy of your complaint

Stage 2: CONCILIATION (30-45 days)
- The Council first attempts conciliation
- Both parties are called for a hearing (can be virtual)
- A conciliator tries to reach a mutual agreement
- If both parties agree, a settlement is recorded

Stage 3: ARBITRATION (if conciliation fails)
- If conciliation fails, the matter moves to arbitration
- Arbitration proceedings are conducted by the Council
- The Council acts as an arbitrator under the Arbitration & Conciliation Act, 1996
- Evidence and arguments are heard from both sides

Stage 4: AWARD/ORDER (within 90 days of filing)
- The Council must dispose of the case within 90 days
- The Council issues an award/order
- The award typically includes:
  - Direction to pay the principal amount
  - Direction to pay interest (at 3× RBI rate)
  - Cost of proceedings (if applicable)

Stage 5: ENFORCEMENT
- The award is enforceable as a decree of court
- If the buyer still doesn't pay, the MSME can approach the Civil Court for execution
- If the buyer wants to appeal, they must deposit 75% of the awarded amount first (Section 19)

IMPORTANT TIMELINES:
- Filing to Notice: 15-30 days
- Conciliation attempt: 30-45 days
- Total resolution: 90 days (mandated by law)
- In practice, cases may take 4-6 months due to procedural delays

TIPS FOR SUCCESSFUL FILING ON SAMADHAAN
------------------------------------------

1. FILE EARLY: Don't wait too long after payment is overdue. File within 60-90 days of the overdue date.

2. MAINTAIN EVIDENCE: Keep all invoices, delivery challans, purchase orders, and email communications. Digital records and timestamps are especially powerful.

3. SEND FORMAL NOTICE FIRST: Before filing on Samadhaan, send a formal demand notice to the buyer via email and registered post. This shows you made reasonable efforts to recover payment.

4. BE ACCURATE: Ensure all invoice details, amounts, and dates are correct. Any discrepancy can delay the case.

5. CALCULATE INTEREST CORRECTLY: Use the correct RBI bank rate and calculate interest accurately from the overdue date. Digital-Vakeel does this automatically.

6. INCLUDE ALL INVOICES: If you have multiple unpaid invoices from the same buyer, you can club them in a single case.

7. KNOW YOUR COUNCIL: Cases are handled by the MSEFC in the buyer's district/state. Each state has its own Facilitation Council.

8. ATTEND HEARINGS: When called for conciliation or arbitration, attend the hearing (or send an authorized representative with a power of attorney).

9. SEEK PROFESSIONAL HELP IF NEEDED: While the process is designed for self-filing, you can engage a lawyer or chartered accountant to assist with complex cases.

10. FOLLOW UP: Check the Samadhaan portal regularly for updates on your case. The portal provides status tracking.

COMMON QUESTIONS ABOUT SAMADHAAN
-----------------------------------

Q: Is there any fee to file on Samadhaan?
A: No, filing on the MSME Samadhaan portal is FREE. There is no court fee or filing fee.

Q: Can I file if I don't have Udyam Registration?
A: No, you must have a valid Udyam Registration to file. Registration is free at https://udyamregistration.gov.in.

Q: Can I file against a government department?
A: Yes, you can file against government departments, PSUs, and any other buyer.

Q: What if the buyer is in a different state?
A: The case is filed with the Facilitation Council in the buyer's state/jurisdiction.

Q: Can I withdraw the case if the buyer pays?
A: Yes, you can withdraw the case at any stage if the payment is received.

Q: What if the buyer doesn't respond to the Council's notice?
A: The Council can proceed ex-parte (without the buyer's presence) and pass an order.

Q: Can the buyer appeal the Council's decision?
A: Yes, but only after depositing 75% of the awarded amount with the court (Section 19, MSMED Act).

Q: How do I track my case status?
A: Login to https://samadhaan.msme.gov.in and check "My Cases" section.

STATISTICS (as of 2024-2025):
- Over 2.5 lakh cases filed on the Samadhaan portal
- Cases involving over ₹30,000 crore in disputed amounts
- Resolution rate: approximately 40-50% resolved through conciliation
- Average resolution time: 90-180 days

MICRO AND SMALL ENTERPRISE FACILITATION COUNCILS (MSEFCs)
-----------------------------------------------------------
Each state/UT has one or more MSEFCs. The Council typically comprises:
- Director of Industries (or nominee) — Chairperson
- Representatives from MSME Associations
- Representatives from banks
- Representatives from buyer associations
- A legal expert/retired judge

The Council has all the powers of a civil court for the purposes of the reference, including:
- Summoning and enforcing attendance of witnesses
- Requiring discovery and production of documents
- Receiving evidence on affidavits
- Issuing commissions for examination of witnesses or documents
